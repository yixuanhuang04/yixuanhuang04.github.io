Beyond Academia

Welcome to this space — a quieter, more personal corner of my website. If my homepage gives you a sense of my academic journey, this page is where I share some of the life around it.

Beyond research, there are walks, books, songs, games, conversations, travels, photographs, ordinary days, and small memories that quietly shape who I am. They may not appear on a CV, but they matter deeply to the person behind it.

Here, I collect fragments of that life: personal essays, photo logs, travel notes, reading reflections, hobbies, memories, and occasional videos — not as a polished portfolio, but as a growing archive of how I live, learn, rest, and remember.

Why Beyond Academia?

You may wonder why an academic homepage has a page like this. Many academic websites are built around research interests, publications, projects, awards, and professional milestones — and for good reason. These are important parts of academic life.

But I have always felt that they are not the whole story. A person is also shaped by the places they have been, the books they read, the people they meet, the hobbies they return to, the questions they carry, and the quiet moments that never become bullet points on a résumé.

I call this page Beyond Academia because I want to record and share the parts of myself that exist beyond academic work. Not because they are separate from who I am as a student or researcher, but because they are part of the same life — the human ground from which curiosity, patience, creativity, and care can grow.

Personal Philosophy

I have long been moved by the following words. I know it is hard to truly live up to them, but they remind me why I keep learning, why I keep trying, and what kind of person I hope to become.

为天地立心 To ordain conscience for Heaven and Earth.
为生民立命 To secure life and fortune for the people.
为往圣继绝学 To continue lost teachings for past sages.
为万世开太平 To establish peace for all future generations.

A Small Closing Note

I once read a line that has never left me: “If my story can be remembered by one more person, I have lived one more life.” Perhaps that is one reason I keep this archive — not to make every moment significant, but to remember that ordinary days can still be worth keeping.

If you are reading this now, thank you for being here. Perhaps one day we will meet at a conference, share an afternoon tea, exchange ideas, travel somewhere, or simply become friends along the way.

朝暮与年岁并往，然后一同与你行至天光。
— As dawn and dusk travel along with the years, may we walk together into the light.
