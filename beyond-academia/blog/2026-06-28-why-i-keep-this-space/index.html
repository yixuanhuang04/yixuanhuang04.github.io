Why I Keep This Space

A note on building a personal website, keeping a space beyond academia, and recording the quieter parts of becoming.

Published Jun. 28, 2026 Updated Jun. 28, 2026 4 min read Viewed times
#blog #essay #reflection #personal archive

Every academic homepage begins with a strange kind of compression. A name, an institution, a degree, a few research interests, selected projects, honors, links, and perhaps a short biography — all arranged neatly so that a visitor can understand, within a few seconds, who this person is professionally.

That kind of page is useful, and I still need it. I want this website to show my academic path clearly: where I study, what I am interested in, what I have worked on, and how people can reach me. But I have also realized that a homepage can quietly become something more than a digital CV. It can become a place where a person leaves traces of how they think, how they grow, and how they choose to remember.

Why a Personal Website?

At first, building a homepage felt practical. It gave me a place to put my academic information, contact links, projects, and updates. Unlike a social media profile, a personal website feels more independent. It is not shaped by a feed, an algorithm, or the speed of constant posting. It can be slower, quieter, and more intentional.

I like that. A homepage gives me a small corner of the web that I can organize myself. It lets me decide what should be highlighted, what should be archived, and what kind of impression I want to leave. In that sense, this website is not only a portfolio. It is also a long-term record of becoming — incomplete, changing, and hopefully more honest over time.

Why Beyond Academia?

You may wonder why an academic homepage has a page called Beyond Academia. Many academic websites do not have such a space, and that is completely understandable. Academic pages are usually built around research, publications, projects, awards, and professional milestones. These things matter. They are part of the work, and they help others understand what a person studies and contributes.

But I have always felt that they are not the whole story. A person is also shaped by the places they have been, the books they read, the games they play, the conversations they remember, the hobbies they return to, and the ordinary days that never become bullet points on a résumé.

Not everything that shapes a person becomes a line on a CV.

For me, Beyond Academia is a way to make room for those parts. It is not separate from who I am as a student or researcher. Rather, it belongs to the same life. The patience needed in research, the curiosity that keeps me learning, the ability to notice details, and the desire to connect with others all grow from experiences that are not always academic in form.

What I Hope to Keep Here

This space may include personal essays, photo logs, travel notes, reading reflections, hobby records, and occasional videos. Some entries may be polished; others may simply be fragments. Some may be about a place I visited, a book that stayed with me, a song I tried to learn on the guitar, a game of tennis, or a quiet thought after a long day.

I do not want this archive to become another performance. I do not want every memory to be turned into a lesson, every hobby into productivity, or every reflection into a perfectly packaged story. I simply want to keep a record of things that matter to me, even if they are small.

There is something meaningful about preserving ordinary moments. Years later, I may not remember every deadline, every task, or every version of a project. But I hope I can still remember what a season felt like, what I was thinking about, what kind of person I was trying to become, and who walked with me along the way.

A Small Beginning

So this first blog post is not a grand announcement. It is more like a small note placed at the entrance of a room: this is why the room exists, and this is what I hope it can hold.

If my homepage tells you what I am doing academically, I hope this space can tell you a little more about how I see, live, learn, and remember. And if you are reading this now, thank you for stepping into this corner of the website. Perhaps one day, a thought, a place, a book, or a memory recorded here will become the beginning of a conversation.
